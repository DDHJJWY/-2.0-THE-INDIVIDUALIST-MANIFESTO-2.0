CHAPTER II: PRINCIPLES OF THE NEW SOCIETY & INDIVIDUAL CULTIVATION
2.1 Core Values

  1. No Absolute "Right or Wrong"
    Except for verifiable physical laws, all "morality" and "ethics" are subjective constructs.
    Morality is a tool for social maintenance, not absolute truth. Law is a mechanism for order, not a sacred entity. Every individual’s perspective has its own internal rationality based on their specific environment and experiences.
    You may dislike or oppose an idea, but you should not simply label it as "wrong." The word "wrong" presupposes an absolute standard, which itself is a human construct.
  1. Supremacy of Strength
    Those who are stronger should naturally possess more resources and power.
    This is not because it "ought to be," but because it is the fundamental law of natural and social operation. The strong can better drive civilizational progress, break through limits, and create greater value.
    The weak must either strive to become strong or accept their current reality. If the weak manage to overthrow the strong, it proves they have become the new "strong" and rightfully deserve to be the new rulers.
  1. Absolute Individual Responsibility
    Each person is solely responsible for themselves.
    Do not expect society or others to assist you. One's background, environment, and starting point are established facts; complaining about them is meaningless. The only valid action is to achieve the best possible outcome within those given conditions.
    Helping others is a personal choice, not an obligation. All interactions must be based on a calculation of interests or personal will, rather than moral coercion (moral kidnapping).
    If you fail, do not blame the world; blame only your own lack of strength.
  1. Honesty and Perceiving the Essence
    The core ability is to strip away false ideologies and see the true nature of things. Most people are shackled by indoctrinated concepts from childhood and never truly question: What is a Nation? What is Law? What is Work? What is Money?
    The primary method for seeing the essence is the "Four-Step Piercing Method":
    Step 1: Individual Impact Layer: How does this affect me? What are the short-term, long-term, direct, indirect, positive, and negative impacts?
    Step 2: Stakeholder Impact Layer: How does this affect people with different positions and interests? Who benefits? Who suffers?
    Step 3: Systemic Impact & Reverse Deduction: What changes does this cause in the larger system (society, industry, organization)? Then, reverse-engineer the motives, interests, emotions, and positions of the people who created and executed it.
    Step 4: Essence Penetration Layer: Strip away all appearances and packaging to see the fundamental reason and true purpose of its existence. Remove emotional filters and propaganda, revealing the raw drivers: Interest, Power, Need, and Fear.
  1. Goal-Orientation
    After seeing the essence, set your goals and never lower your standards.
    A true goal is what you genuinely want after seeing through all illusions, not something driven by social narratives or emotional impulses.
    Within the bounds of objective reality, do not abandon your goal due to emotion, fear, or social pressure. If objective conditions truly make a goal impossible (e.g., physical laws or age limits), you may adjust your direction. This is not "lowering standards," but a "rational adjustment."
    Every step should be the optimal choice under current conditions, leaving no room for regret. Regret stems from self-deception—claiming something was "impossible" when you actually gave up out of fear.
  1. Instrumental Rationality
    Nations, laws, morality, and even people are tools.
    Nothing should be treated as sacred or inviolable. If a better tool exists, it should be replaced. Everything must serve the ultimate goal.
    This is not cold-bloodedness; it is rationality. True progress comes from the constant optimization of tools, not from clinging to tradition.
  1. Openness and Evolution
    Always welcome better ideas and always be prepared to change.
    Systems can be modified at any time, concepts updated, and methods optimized. Oppose pedantry and stagnation; pursue value, strength, and efficiency.
    Anything that does not align with these three should be improved. We even encourage the overthrow of existing systems, provided the challenger is truly stronger and possesses a superior plan.
    2.2 The Core of Individual Cultivation: Tong-Xin-Ming-Xing (Transparent Heart & Lucid Nature)
    What is Tong-Xin-Ming-Xing?
    It is a state of achieving total transparency and rational control over all of one's emotions, desires, fears, pains, and external stimuli.
    This does not mean becoming an emotionless robot. On the contrary, it requires you to feel all emotions deeply but remain able to analyze and master them rationally.
    Most people suffer because they either suppress emotions (pretending they don't exist), are controlled by them (acting impulsively), or avoid them (numbing themselves). This cultivation requires you to neither suppress, avoid, nor be controlled.
    Tong-Xin (Transparent Heart): Fluidity and Transparency
    Allow any internal fluctuation—fear, anger, desire, pain, joy—to arise naturally and be fully experienced.
    Do not block, suppress, or escape. When an emotion arrives, do not tell yourself "I shouldn't feel this." Let it flow through you completely.
    The Standard: When a fluctuation occurs, you can clearly perceive its full intensity and detail without omission, distortion, or self-numbing.
    The Purpose: To prevent emotions from accumulating in the shadows, which creates larger interferences later. Suppressed emotions do not disappear; they eventually explode in a more intense and uncontrollable manner.
    Ming-Xing (Lucid Nature): Clear Insight
    Every emotional fluctuation must be dissected rationally:
    Origin: Does it come from biological instinct (hunger, libido, fatigue)? Childhood experience (trauma or conditioned reflex)? Social indoctrination (what you were taught to feel)? Or a current environmental stimulus?
    Mechanism: What is the causal chain? Why did this specific stimulus trigger this emotion? What psychological process occurred in between?
    Essence: Is it a protective mechanism (fear guarding you from danger)? Resource consumption (anxiety draining your energy)? Goal support (motivation driving you forward)? Or an obstacle (envy hindering rational judgment)?
    The Standard: The dissection must be objective and verifiable, without self-deception or moral sugarcoating. Many people make excuses for their emotions, calling fear "caution" or laziness "a need for rest." This is self-deception.
    The Purpose: To transform emotion from a "mysterious uncontrollable force" into "known data." Once you fully understand the source, mechanism, and essence of an emotion, it loses its power over you.
    The Ultimate Goal of Tong-Xin-Ming-Xing:
    This is not for pursuing "inner peace," but for reclaiming the scepter of power. In the old society, emotions are cheap levers used by rulers to manipulate the masses—fear ensures obedience, vanity drives consumption, and hypocritical sympathy leads to self-sacrifice. A person who has not undergone this wash of "Lucid Nature" is essentially an automaton driven by biological instincts and social commands.
    Signs of Achievement:
    Absolute Transparency: When anger or fear arises, the individual observes it like a chemical reaction in a laboratory. It no longer creates "unknown interference."
    Purity of Decision-making: All decisions are stripped of emotional impurities. Emotions become data points on a dashboard, while the Highest Goal holds the steering wheel.
    Immunity to External Pressure: When society attempts to besiege the individual with "shame" or "moral pressure," the Awakened can instantly perceive its nature as a "tool of governance," rendering the pressure null and void.
    Thus, Tong-Xin-Ming-Xing is not just mental exercise; it is the armament of the Individualist. Only when a person is no longer enslaved by their internal undercurrents are they truly qualified to challenge the old world and build the new society.
    The Bottom-Logic Audit:
    One must clearly see that all emotions are ultimately biochemical feedbacks based on Interests (Survival, Reproduction, and Psychological Security). Fear protects your physical assets; anger gains advantages in resource competition through bravado; shame is a "security code" implanted by social systems for low-cost domestication. If you can instantly perceive the interest behind an emotion, simple emotional fluctuations become meaningless in the face of logic—they are just dancing data on a screen, not commands to be followed.
